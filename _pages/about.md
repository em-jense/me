Hello, welcome to my portfolio! My name is Emily Jensen, and I am currently finishing up my MLIS degree at the University of Alberta. Before this, I bounced from a Bachelor of Arts in Media Studies at the University of British Colombia with plans for journalism to coming back home to Alberta to actually finish my undergraduate degree in Bachelor of Arts in Women's and Gender Studies (plus a minor in Classics). While my academic pursuits evolved over the years, at the core of it was an interest in and passion for social critique and social change – to see what the world is, but also what it could be. 
This core interest has blended with research into body politics, representation, media criticism, and other critical theories primarily rooted in critical feminist and queer studies. My practices as a scholar have primarily focused on traditional academic production, with papers and literature/content analysis with some discourse analysis as well. But the introduction to digital humanities has inspired me to look more into research creation/critical making as a means of expressing ideas. This has been particularly important as I become interested in independent scholarship and becoming more emersed in how stories and creative works can be used to work through and interogate 'real world' issues and systems.
Alongside this, I also volunteer at the Royal Alberta Museum with the botany department (previously) and the collections team (currently). I am also a co-producer of Shout! for Libaries, a radio show hosted through CJSR 88.5FM with a focus on the twists and turns of the field of librarianship.
